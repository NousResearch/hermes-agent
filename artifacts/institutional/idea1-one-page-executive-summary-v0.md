# Idea 1 One-Page Executive Summary v0

## BTC Volatility Intermediation Desk

**Institutional BTC risk transformation across ETF, CME, Deribit, and OTC markets.**

---

## Thesis

Bitcoin exposure has fragmented across institutional wrappers: spot BTC ETFs, ETF options, CME futures/options, crypto-native options venues, and OTC derivatives. These wrappers reference similar economic exposure but trade through different margin systems, collateral rules, custody constraints, legal regimes, participant bases, and natural client flows.

That fragmentation creates an opportunity to build an institutional BTC risk-intermediation platform that helps treasuries, miners, funds, and allocators transform BTC volatility into income, protection, and structured risk.

The business should not launch as a generic “BTC options arbitrage fund.” The stronger wedge is:

> Board-ready BTC treasury and miner hedging programs, powered by a proprietary cross-venue BTC volatility monitor.

---

## Why now

- Spot BTC ETFs and listed ETF options created a TradFi-accessible BTC options channel.
- CME BTC futures/options provide regulated futures-market infrastructure.
- Deribit remains the deep crypto-native BTC volatility surface.
- Public BTC treasury companies and miners now have real BTC balance-sheet and cash-flow exposure.
- Corporate holders and miners need income, floors, runway protection, and board-governed risk policies.
- Most institutions cannot directly optimize across ETF, CME, Deribit, and OTC plumbing.

---

## Product menu

### 1. BTC treasury covered-call program
Small, board-approved sleeve of BTC/ETF exposure used to generate recurring premium income while keeping the majority of BTC uncapped.

### 2. BTC treasury collar / put-spread collar
Defined downside floor or protected drawdown band financed partly by selling upside on a minority sleeve.

### 3. Miner production hedge
Conservative 25–50% hedge on expected production using puts, collars, or put-spread collars to protect power, capex, and debt-service runway.

### 4. Cross-venue vol spread package
ETF-vs-Deribit-vs-CME relative-value analytics, skew/calendar comparisons, and quote-verified dislocation monitoring.

### 5. Structured products / defined-outcome notes
Later-stage BTC-linked yield/upside/protection products for private banks, structured-product desks, and qualified allocators.

## Initial evidence

A first screen-only monitor showed:

- Deribit BTC options surface accessible through public API.
- IBIT option-chain data accessible for research through public Nasdaq JSON, though production requires OPRA/vendor data.
- BlackRock/iShares holdings data supports BTC-per-share normalization.
- CME is strategically required but needs licensed/vendor/broker data for production.
- OTC economics remain hypothesis until quote-verified.

Initial screen-only observations showed IBIT near-dated ATM IV appearing richer than Deribit in the sample, but this is not yet executable arbitrage proof.

The correct standard is:

> Hypothesis → screen-only → quote-verified → trade-verified.

No return claim should be made until quote/trade verification survives bid/ask, fees, basis, margin, collateral, and legal/operational constraints.

## Commercial wedge

### First wedge: BTC treasury companies

Targets include BTC treasury companies, operating companies with BTC reserves, and BTC-heavy miners.

Pain points:
- dilution from equity/convert issuance;
- preferred/coupon/refi obligations;
- operating runway;
- NAV volatility;
- need to avoid selling core BTC.

Hook:

> Monetize a capped, board-approved slice of BTC convexity to fund obligations or reduce dilution, while preserving strategic BTC ownership.

### Second wedge: Bitcoin miners

Targets include public/private miners with production, inventory, power, capex, and debt exposure.

Pain points:
- USD power/capex liabilities;
- BTC revenue volatility;
- hashprice compression;
- debt/refi/lender pressure;
- forced-sale risk.

Hook:

> Protect power, capex, and debt-service runway by collaring conservative expected production while preserving most BTC upside.

## Operating model

### Phase 1 — Research and analytics
Build the BTC Vol Desk Monitor, normalize surfaces, produce screen-only dislocation board, and maintain evidence appendix.

### Phase 2 — Advisory / structuring
Develop board-ready treasury/miner hedge policies, illustrative term sheets, calculators, and governance packages.

### Phase 3 — RFQ verification
Collect quote evidence through legally approved counterparties/partners; compare all-in economics to screen models.

### Phase 4 — Execution / risk intermediation
Only after counsel-approved wrapper: execution partnerships, principal sleeve, risk warehousing, and hedge recycling.

### Phase 5 — Structured products
Later-stage defined-outcome BTC products with issuer/distribution/legal framework.

## Legal discipline

The MVP must remain research/analytics/illustrative until counsel defines the wrapper.

Client-specific advice, live RFQs, transaction compensation, principal dealing, and structured-product distribution may trigger SEC/CFTC/NFA/FINRA/RIA/broker-dealer/CTA/CPO/IB/FCM/swap-dealer issues.

Clean MVP:

- research;
- analytics;
- illustrative case studies;
- legal perimeter design;
- quote-verification planning.

Not clean without counsel:

- quoting public-company derivatives;
- taking transaction compensation;
- introducing orders;
- advising specific clients;
- distributing BTC-linked notes.
