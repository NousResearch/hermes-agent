## PART 4 — FOOD TRUCKS / STREET FOOD (Truk'i Pan Culture)

---

### Bonaire Food Truck Scene — General
Bonaire has a local tradition called **"Truk'i Pan"** (literally "bread truck") — local food trucks and small stands that are a beloved part of the culture. Most operate in the evenings, often near the waterfront in Kralendijk or at community gathering spots. They serve:
- **Local dishes**: Kabritu stoba (goat stew), Keshi Yena (cheese stuffed with spiced fish/meat), conch, fried fish, funchi (polenta), pastechis (fried pastries filled with meat/shrimp/fish)
- **BBQ meats**, grilled chicken, ribs
- Look for signs: **"Aki ta Bende Kuminda Krioyo"** = "Local food sold here"

### 15. Truk'i Pan – Roaming/Pop-up Food Trucks
- **Locations**: Vary — commonly near Kaya Grandi (main street), Wilhelminaplein (central square), Bonaire's waterfront, and Isidel Beach Park area
- **Hours**: Typically evenings, 6:00 PM–midnight (varies widely)
- **Price Range**: $ (very affordable — large portions, local prices)
- **Notes**: The best authentic local food experience. Portions are huge and economical. Look for:
  - Pastechis (fried pastries) — great snack
  - Sopi (soups), especially fish soup
  - Fried fish / piska
  - Goat stew (kabritu stoba)
  - Chicken with funchi
  - Ask for pika siboyo sauce (onions in vinegar + hot peppers) — the classic accompaniment
  - Order mitar-mitar (half-and-half: rice + funchi) for an authentic combo

## PART 7 — DINING TIPS FOR YOUR TRIP

### Practical Notes
- **No fast food chains** on Bonaire (verified by InfoBonaire) — no McDonald's, Burger King, etc.
- **Reservations**: For waterside or popular spots, book 1–2 days ahead. Front desk of your accommodation can help.
- **Service is slow by design** — kitchens cook fresh. Budget 2+ hours for a proper sit-down dinner.
- **Check is not delivered automatically** — you must ask for it when ready to leave.
- **10% service charge** sometimes included (+ 6% tax); if not, 15–20% tip is appreciated.
- **ATMs** within walking distance of most center-of-town restaurants.
- **Sunday closures**: Many independent restaurants close Sunday or reduce hours. Some resort restaurants (Delfins) are resort-guests-only on Sundays.

### What to Try (Must-Order List)
1. **Keshi Yena** — Bonaire's signature dish: cheese stuffed with spiced fish/meat
2. **Kabritu Stoba** — Goat stew, deeply savory, local staple
3. **Wahoo** — local fresh fish, often grilled or pan-seared
4. **Pastechis** from a Truk'i Pan — best savory snack on the island
5. **Funchi** — polenta accompaniment, excellent with stew
6. **Local lobster** — in season and prepared Caribbean-style
7. **Fresh conch** (karko) — best at local spots, not from chain menus
8. **Piña colada or local rum cocktails** — ubiquitous at beachside bars

### June–July Specific Notes
- Low/shoulder season: tourist crowds are lower, service may be faster
- Some restaurants may have limited days or hours compared to December–April high season
- Weather: very hot (~30°C/86°F), low humidity, strong trade winds — outdoor dining is comfortable in the evening
- Check tourismbonaire.com/events-calendar for any culinary events during your trip dates
