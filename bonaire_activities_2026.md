# Bonaire Activities — Knowledge Bank (2026)

**Compiled:** 2026-05-27 | **Trip Window:** June 26 – July 6, 2026
**Sources:** InfoBonaire.com, BuddyDive.com, STINAPA Bonaire (stinapabonaire.org), Tourism Corporation Bonaire (tourismbonaire.com), KitersParadise.com (bonairekiteschool.com)

## 🚨 Critical Alerts

- **Tourist Entry Tax:** $75 USD per person, per visit — required upon arrival
- **Nature Fee (STINAPA):** $40 USD per person per calendar year — required for all marine and Washington Slagbaai park use. Purchase online at stinapabonaire.org
- **SCTLD Coral Disease:** Stony Coral Tissue Loss Disease active on Bonaire reefs. Disinfect ALL gear before first entry and after last. No gloves, no pointer sticks.
- **Washington Slagbaai Park:** OPEN for hiking/biking/touring — Tue–Sun, 8AM–5PM (last entry 2:30PM), closed Mondays and holidays. 4WD vehicle required. **NO DIVING ALLOWED** in Washington Slagbaai waters.
- **Salt Pier diving:** Closed when salt ships loading (~2-3×/week). Check dive shop day-of.
- **Town Pier diving:** Requires advance permission from harbormaster — arrange through local operator.
- **ATVs / Parasailing:** Neither activity is listed or advertised by any official Bonaire tourism source, InfoBonaire, STINAPA, or major resort operators as of May 2026. These activities do **not** appear to be commercially available on Bonaire. Verify locally before trip.

## June–July Conditions (relevant to outdoor activities)

- **Water temp:** 27–28°C (81–82°F)
- **Visibility (water):** 18–30m — excellent
- **Wind:** ENE trade winds 15–20 knots — consistent and reliable (peak windsurfing/kite season)
- **Rain:** Short afternoon showers, not disruptive
- **Hurricane risk:** Minimal — Bonaire is south of hurricane belt
- **Air temp:** ~29°C / 84°F
- **Windsurf/kite season note:** June–August is HIGH WIND season — ideal for Lac Bay and Atlantis Beach

## 1. WATER ACTIVITIES — DIVING & SNORKELING

### 1.1 Shore Scuba Diving
**Category:** Water — Diving
**Location:** West coast of Bonaire, all marked with yellow painted rocks. 42 shore-accessible sites from Karpata (north) to Red Slave (south)
**Price Range:** $$–$$$
- Tank fill: ~$12–15 USD (air), ~$15–18 (Nitrox) — per dive
- Equipment rental (BCD/reg/wetsuit): ~$25–40/day typical
- Nature Tag: $40/year (required, one-time)
**Notes:**
- Self-guided shore diving is the #1 activity. Rent a truck and load tanks in the bed — no car needed for a dive boat.
- Must-do sites: Hilma Hooker (wreck), 1000 Steps, Karpata, Alice in Wonderland, Oil Slick Leap, Something Special (macro)
- 66 total sites charted; yellow rocks mark entry points along the coastal road
- STINAPA BNMP rules: buddy system mandatory, no anchoring, max depth 60m
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★★ — This is Bonaire's #1 draw. Multiple dives daily are standard.

---

### 1.2 Boat Diving — Klein Bonaire
**Category:** Water — Diving
**Location:** Klein Bonaire (islet ~800m offshore, west of Kralendijk)
**Price Range:** $$$
- 2-tank boat trip: ~$70–100+ per person (varies by operator)
**Notes:**
- Klein Bonaire is uninhabited and protected. 12 boat-only sites including Mi Dushi, Hands Off, Just a Nice Dive, Keepsake
- Best wall dives and turtle encounters. Crystal-clear water.
- Book through any major dive shop: Buddy Dive, Dive Friends, Let's Go Dive Bonaire
- No Beach/Day access from shore — boat only
- Also accessible as a snorkel day trip
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★★ — Must-do at least once, ideally 2–3 times.

---

### 1.3 Night Diving
**Category:** Water — Diving
**Location:** Multiple sites: Town Pier (world-class), Salt Pier, Something Special, 1000 Steps, Oil Slick Leap
**Price Range:** $$–$$$
- Similar to day dive rates; guided night dives ~$40–60 extra depending on operator
**Notes:**
- Town Pier is considered one of the **world's top night dives** — pilings covered in sponges, cup corals; abundant tarpon, frogfish, nudibranchs. Requires advance harbormaster permission through a local dive operator.
- Salt Pier: stunning at night, but check ship schedule
- Night gear (torch/SMB) usually rentable
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★★ — At least 1–2 night dives highly recommended.

---

### 1.4 Shore Snorkeling
**Category:** Water — Snorkeling
**Location:** Any of the shore dive sites along west coast. Best snorkeling: 18th Palm, Bachelor's Beach, Pink Beach, Tori's Reef, Yellow Sub, 1000 Steps terrace
**Price Range:** $–$$
- Gear rental: ~$10–15/day
- No guide needed — follow yellow rocks, wade in
- Nature fee: $40 (covers all water entry)
**Notes:**
- Shallow reef terrace 1–5m deep harbors 75+ fish species visible without diving
- No boat or certification needed — family-friendly
- Pink Beach: one of the most beautiful snorkel spots; parking area, walk in
- 1000 Steps: great shallow terrace above the wall
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — Perfect for non-divers or as a surface break.

---

### 1.5 Guided Snorkel Boat Trip — Klein Bonaire
**Category:** Water — Snorkeling
**Location:** Klein Bonaire, departure from Kralendijk waterfront
**Price Range:** $$–$$$
- Typical guided snorkel trip: $40–70/person
**Notes:**
- Buddy Dive offers a dedicated Klein Bonaire snorkel boat for guests
- Best way for non-divers/beginners to see the reefs around Klein Bonaire
- Guides point out sea turtles, eagle rays, reef fish
- Often combined with island walk around Klein Bonaire
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — Best snorkel experience on the island.

## 2. WATER ACTIVITIES — ON THE WATER

### 2.1 Kayaking — Mangrove Tours
**Category:** Water — Kayaking
**Location:** Lac Bay mangroves, east coast; also along west coast from resorts
**Price Range:** $$
- Guided mangrove kayak tour: ~$30–60/person
- Self-guided kayak rental: ~$15–25/hour
**Notes:**

- Combine with snorkeling trip for full day
**Recommended Operator:** Mangrove Info & Kayak Center +599 780-5353
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — One of the most popular non-diving activities.

### 2.2 Windsurfing
**Category:** Water — Windsurfing
**Location:** Lac Bay, east coast of Bonaire (the primary hub); also available at some resorts on west coast
**Price Range:** $$–$$$
- Board + sail rental: ~$60–100/day
- Beginner lessons: ~$50–80/hour (group), $80–120 (private)
**Notes:**
- Lac Bay is world-class — 90% chance of favorable ENE trade winds, 8 sq km protected bay free of boat traffic
- High wind season is June–August: perfect for June 26–July 6 trip
- Beginners: shallow knee-deep water for learning, protective barrier reef
- Intermediate: middle of bay, moderate conditions
- Advanced: outer edges of Lac Bay, high swells and strong wind
- Windsurf shops located right on the bay edge — top equipment + all levels of instruction
- Sorobon Beach area is the windsurfing center
- Buddy Dive schedules weekly introductory classes with local outfitters for their guests
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★★ — Peak season for this trip. World-class conditions.

### 2.5 Sport Fishing (Deep-Sea Charter)
**Category:** Water — Fishing
**Location:** Waters off west coast and open ocean, departure from Kralendijk
**Price Range:** $$$$ (whole boat)
- Charter boats typically priced per boat, not per person: ~$400–800/half day (estimated — verify with operators)
- For groups of 4–6 people, per-person cost becomes more reasonable
**Species:** Marlin, Sailfish, Yellowfin Tuna, Wahoo, Dorado (Mahi Mahi)
**Notes:**
- Mostly catch-and-release; confirm with captain if you want to keep fish
- Most guests fish early morning (pre-dawn baitfish catch, then game fishing)
- Fishing license NOT required for non-commercial fishing
- Non-residents cannot sell catch (Bonaire law)
- Fresh fish cannot be imported into US, Holland, etc. — cannot bring home
**Recommended Operator:** Piscatur: +599-717-8774 or +599-780-8774 or +599 780-0833
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★☆☆ — Niche but excellent for fishing enthusiasts.

## 3. LAND ACTIVITIES — NATURE & ADVENTURE

### 3.1 Hiking — Washington Slagbaai National Park
**Category:** Land — Hiking / Nature
**Location:** Washington Slagbaai National Park, northwest tip of Bonaire
**Hours:** Tuesday–Sunday, 8AM–5PM (last entry 2:30PM). **Closed Mondays and national holidays.**
**Price Range:** $ (covered by Nature Fee)
- Nature Fee: $40 USD/person/year (same fee as marine park — covers both)
- Park is CASHLESS — pay online at stinapabonaire.org or by card at gate
**Three Established Trails:**

1. **Lagadishi Walking Trail** (~2 hours, EASY)
   - Limestone plateau with full Bonairean windward scenery
   - Flamingos frequently seen in the salt pan
   - Features: historic sites, cacti, mangroves, blow-holes, sand dunes, oceanic beach
   
2. **Kasikunda Climbing Trail** (45 minutes, CHALLENGING)
   - Steep volcanic hill climb from Visitor Center
   - Uneven terrain, strenuous
   - Reward: best view of north Bonaire from Malmok to Spelonk
   
3. **Subi Brandaris Trail** (45 minutes, MODERATE–CHALLENGING)
   - Summit: 241m (790 feet) — highest point on Bonaire
   - Panoramic views across the entire island
   - Start early morning — cooler temps, more wildlife

**General notes:**
- 4WD vehicle recommended to reach trailheads (dirt roads)
- Bring plenty of water, sunscreen, hat, good hiking shoes
- Wildlife: flamingos, iguanas, parrots, parakeets, sea turtles (beach nesting)
- Cultural: former plantation ruins, visitor center museum
- **NO DIVING** in Washington Slagbaai waters
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — Top-tier nature experience, especially Subi Brandaris for views.

### 3.2 Mountain Biking / Cycling
**Category:** Land — Biking
**Location:** Island-wide: kunuku (outback) trails, coastal roads, Washington Slagbaai, Rincon Valley, Bara di Karta trails. Marked with painted stone markers.
**Price Range:** $–$$
- Bike rental: ~$20–35/day (TREK, Giant, mountain bikes at shops in Kralendijk and some resorts)
- Guided tour: ~$40–80/person (half-day or full-day)
**Notes:**
- Hundreds of miles of off-road trails with painted stone markers
- Buddy Dive organizes guided tours matching fitness levels
- Routes include kunuku (outback), Gotomeer (flamingo lake), Rincon village (oldest settlement on Bonaire)
- Washington Slagbaai National Park has mountain biking trails
- Many guided routes include picnic and swim/snorkel stops along coast
- Annual Duo Xtreme Mountain Bike Race confirms established trail network
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★☆☆ — Good topside activity; best done in morning (cool). June–July heat can be a factor in midday.

### 3.3 Birdwatching
**Category:** Land — Nature
**Location:** Island-wide. Best spots: Washington Slagbaai NP, Gotomeer (salt lake), Lac Bay, Pink Beach / south salt pans, Willemstoren Lighthouse area
**Price Range:** $–$$
- Self-guided: Free (only Nature Fee if in parks)
- Guided birding tour with local guide: ~$50–100/person (half-day)
**Notes:**
- 190+ bird species recorded on Bonaire
- **American Flamingo** — Bonaire's national bird; seen at Gotomeer, Lac Bay, Washington Slagbaai, south salt pans. Flocks at sunset near Willemstoren.
- **Yellow-shouldered Amazon Parrot ("Lora")** — Bonaire subspecies, protected. Found at sunrise/sunset in northern sections.
- Part of the Caribbean Birding Trail (CBT)
- **Local guide:** Elsmarie Beukenboom: +599 786-6016 | ebeukenboom@gmail.com (English, Dutch, Spanish)
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★☆☆ — June–July: flamingos visible at multiple sites. Great for early-morning outings before heat.

### 3.4 Washington Slagbaai National Park — Car Touring
**Category:** Land — Sightseeing / Nature Drive
**Location:** Washington Slagbaai National Park, northwest Bonaire
**Price Range:** $ (Nature Fee)
- $40/year covers all park use
**Notes:**
- 4WD required — dirt roads, rocky terrain
- Two routes: Short Route and Long Route — both lead to Slagbaai beach/lagoon
- Highlights: spectacular views, historic sites, diverse cacti, divi-divi trees, iguanas, flamingos at close range at Slagbaai lagoon
- Can be combined with hiking (Subi Brandaris) and birdwatching in same day
- Visitor Center + Museum at park entrance
- Cabins/camping available by reservation at Slagbaai lagoon
- Bring food and plenty of water — no restaurant in park
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — Full-day excursion. Best done early to avoid heat.

## 4. UNIQUE & CULTURAL ACTIVITIES

### 4.1 Cadushy Distillery Tour
**Category:** Cultural — Unique Tour
**Location:** Rincon (or nearby) — check cadushyofliqueur.com for address
**Price Range:** $–$$
**Notes:**
- The Cadushy Distillery is Bonaire's only distillery
- Tours of the distilling process; tastings of Cadushy of Bonaire liqueur made from the kadusji cactus (endemic to Bonaire)
- InfoBonaire and BuddyDive both recommend as a "must-do" unique experience
- Makes a distinctive local souvenir
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★☆☆ — Fun afternoon activity; authentic local product.

---

### 4.2 Sea Turtle Conservation — STCB Beach Walks
**Category:** Cultural — Conservation Experience
**Location:** Klein Bonaire beaches (primary nesting site); accessible by boat
**Price Range:** $–$$
- Typically a small fee or donation
**Operator:** STCB (Sea Turtle Conservation Bonaire) — stcb.org
**Notes:**
- **May through October**: Join STCB experts on nightly/dawn beach walks to monitor sea turtle nests on Klein Bonaire
- Trip dates June 26–July 6 fall **squarely in peak sea turtle nesting season** — green and loggerhead turtles active
- Participants look for fresh nests made by females and monitor for hatchlings on their way to sea
- Meaningful conservation participation, not just observation
- Must book in advance with STCB
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★★ — PERFECT timing. Rare hands-on wildlife conservation. Book early.

---

### 4.3 Reef Renewal Foundation — Coral Restoration
**Category:** Cultural — Conservation / Diving
**Location:** Various reef sites around Bonaire
**Price Range:** $$ (typically a donation or session fee)
**Operator:** Reef Renewal Foundation Bonaire — reefrenewalfoundation.org
**Notes:**
- Divers can participate directly in coral restoration: planting coral fragments, maintaining nurseries
- Active response to SCTLD coral disease impact
- Requires scuba certification
- Highly meaningful given SCTLD alert on Bonaire reefs
**Trip Relevance:** ★★★★☆ — Excellent add-on for certified divers who care about reef conservation.
