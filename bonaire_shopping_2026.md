# 🛍️ Bonaire Local Shopping Guide — June 26–July 6, 2026
**STRICT FILTER: Locally owned & island-made only. No chain stores. No mass-produced China imports.**

---

## 🚨 KEY ALERTS FOR TRIP DATES (June 26–July 6, 2026)

- **Low/shoulder season**: Many locally owned shops have **reduced hours** in June–July vs. Dec–April high season. Confirm hours before visiting.
- **Shopping Night** (monthly event, held on Kaya Grandi): Usually 1st or 2nd Friday of each month, 6:00–9:00 PM. Check Tourism Corporation Bonaire events calendar closer to trip for exact dates in your window.
- **Some artisan vendors are appointment-based or sell via Instagram only** — call or DM in advance.
- **Currency**: US Dollars are the official currency of Bonaire (Bonaire is a special municipality of the Netherlands / Caribbean Netherlands). No currency exchange needed for US visitors.
- **Visitor Entry Tax**: $75 per person per visit (required, pay via official site up to 7 days before arrival).

### 1. 🌵 The Cadushy Distillery *(Top Pick — Must Visit)*
**Type:** Artisan distillery / spirits producer / gift shop
**Address:** Kaya Cornelis D. Crestian 8 & 10, Rincon (Bonaire's oldest village)
**Phone:** +599 701-7011
**Website:** www.cadushy.com
**Email:** info@cadushy.com
**Hours:** Monday–Sunday, 10:00 AM–5:00 PM (last tour starts 4:45 PM)
**Entry:** Free — just show up

**What They Sell:**
- **Cadushy of Bonaire Liqueur** — The flagship product. Made from local cactus and lime using Bonairean traditions. Gold/award-winning. Signature "Sunny Taste of Bonaire."
- **Rom Rincón** — Award-winning spiced Caribbean rum named after Bonaire's old capital (Rom Rincón Private Stock and standard). Gold medal, World Spirit Competition 2017 (San Francisco). Small batch, artisan production.
- **Captain Don's Whisky** — Caribbean whisky named after Bonaire diving legend Captain Don Stewart. Silver medal, World Spirit Competition 2017.
- **Cadushy Vodka** — Gold medal, World Spirit Competition 2017 (San Francisco). Made in small batches approved by the master distiller.
- **Salú Caribbean Gin** — Made from 13 botanicals including aloe vera and Yerba di Holé (local basil). 4 years in recipe development.
- **Tripple B Beer** — Locally brewed cactus beer, unique to Bonaire
- **Island Liqueur Series** — Unique liqueurs for each Dutch Caribbean island: Cadushy (Bonaire), Kukui (Aruba), Calbas (Curaçao), Gold of Statia, Spices of Saba
- **Cactus tea**, tumblers, shot glasses, T-shirts, gift sets
- **Mini bottles** ($15 each) — perfect souvenirs / stocking stuffers

**Why It's Worth Visiting:**
The only distillery on Bonaire, founded by Eric Gietman and the Gietman family in 2009. Located in the historic Cinelandia Cinema building in the heart of Rincon, surrounded by ancient Calbas trees, lora parrots, and hummingbirds. They produce everything in small batches on-site. Part of sales goes to the Bonaire Heritage Foundation. Receives ~25,000 visitors annually — a true cultural anchor for the village of Rincon. Free guided tour walks you through the distillation process, followed by tasting in the shady garden courtyard.

**Trip Tip:** Plan a half-day trip to Rincon — combine with the Rincon Valley trails and a local lunch. The "distillery experience" is one of the most unique, authentically Bonairean activities on the island.

### 10. 🏪 Locally Owned Gift Shops — Downtown Kralendijk
**General Category (multiple shops)**
**Location:** Kaya Grandi and waterfront area, Kralendijk

The following types of locally owned shops exist along Kaya Grandi and should be explored on foot:

**Small gift/souvenir shops selling locally sourced items:**
- Locally made ceramic items
- Bonaire-branded items made regionally (not China mass-imports)
- Local photography cards and prints
- Bonaire cookbooks and locally authored books
- Hand-woven items from local craftspeople

**What to Avoid (per research brief filter):**
- Shops selling identical "island-branded" items clearly mass-produced in Asia
- Chain jewelry stores (several exist in the tourist area)
- Stores carrying identical merchandise to what you'd find in any Caribbean tourist port

**How to Identify Locally Owned:**
- Ask the shopkeeper directly: "Is this made on Bonaire?" and "Who made this?"
- Look for handwritten price tags, non-standardized displays, local language (Papiamento) labels
- If the shopkeeper doesn't know the origin of the item, it's likely not local

## PART 5: WHERE TO SHOP — GEOGRAPHY GUIDE

### Kralendijk Town Center
- **Kaya Grandi** (main street): walk the full length, including side alleys
- **Waterfront promenade** (Kaya J.N.E. Craane): Karel's Beach Bar area, small vendors
- **Harborside shops**: a few locally owned small shops between the cruise terminal and town center

### Rincon Village (north of island, ~20 min drive from Kralendijk)
- **The Cadushy Distillery** — THE must-visit for local products
- Small village shops (local hardware/food stores, not tourist shops but authentic)
- Worth exploring the village on foot — some local artisans sell from home

### Lac Bay / East Coast
- **Mangrove Info Center** — conservation gifts
- **Sorobon area** — some windsurfing/kitesurfing vendors (locally operated)

### Resort Shops
- **Buddy Dive Resort, Delfins Beach Resort, Captain Don's Habitat, Carib Inn**: all have small shops. Carib Inn's shop in particular is locally curated and family-run.

## PART 6: SHOPPING TIPS FOR THE LOCAL/ARTISAN FILTER

1. **Ask "Who made this?" and "Is it from Bonaire?"** — The best shops will have direct answers.
2. **Visit Rincon** — Bonaire's oldest village is the cultural heart of the island and home to the only distillery. It's a half-day trip worth doing.
3. **Friday Shopping Night**: Shops in Kralendijk stay open until 9 PM on the monthly shopping night. A lively, local atmosphere.
4. **MoltenWolf Glass is the best for jewelry** — contact via Facebook before visiting in shoulder season.
5. **Avoid the duty-free and jewelry chain strip near the cruise terminal** — these are exactly what the brief filters out.
6. **Cadushy mini-bottles** ($15 each) make excellent, completely unique gifts that pass any customs allowance.
7. **Ellen Muller calendars**: order online (ellenmuller.com) or ask at local dive shops — available in physical form on-island.
8. **Papiamento** is the local language; if a vendor or shopkeeper switches to Papiamento, you're in the right place.
9. **June–July is shoulder season** — fewer tourist crowds, more authentic local shopping experience, but some artisan vendors may take vacation or have limited hours.
10. **Cash is helpful** for small market stalls; USD accepted everywhere.
