## Four-Tier Licensing Model

The Burgess Principle uses a four-tier licensing structure designed to ensure that the framework is accessible to all organisations while maintaining sustainable development and governance.

---

### Tier 1 — Global Technology Companies

**Scope:** Large-scale technology companies deploying AI, automated decision-making, or data processing systems at global scale.

**Fee Structure:**
- **0.01% – 0.1% of relevant revenue** — calculated on revenue derived from products, services, or operations where the Burgess Principle is applied or where certification is sought.

**Rationale:** Global technology companies operate at a scale where the impact of treating individuals as units is most acute. The revenue-based fee ensures proportionate contribution while remaining a negligible fraction of overall revenue.

---

### Tier 2 — Corporate / Enterprise

**Scope:** Businesses and enterprises adopting the Burgess Principle for internal processes, products, or services.

**Fee Structure:**
- **£1,000 one-time licensing fee** plus an **annual maintenance fee** for continued certification and access to updated guidance.

**Rationale:** A fixed fee makes adoption predictable and accessible for mid-size organisations. The annual component ensures ongoing compliance support and framework updates.

---

### Tier 3 — Public Sector

**Scope:** Government bodies, public services, NHS trusts, local authorities, regulators, educational institutions, and non-profit organisations serving the public interest.

**Fee Structure:**
- **Nil** — no licensing fee.

**Rationale:** Public sector organisations serve the public. Charging them to adopt a framework that protects individuals from being processed as units would be counterproductive. Free access ensures the Burgess Principle can be adopted where it is most needed — in the systems that directly affect citizens' lives.

---

### Tier 4 — Litigation Firms

**Scope:** Law firms, advocacy organisations, and legal practitioners using the Burgess Principle as a framework in litigation, regulatory complaints, or enforcement actions.

**Fee Structure:**
- **£1 or nil licensing fee** plus a **2–5% royalty** on any damages, settlements, or awards obtained in cases where the Burgess Principle framework is used as the basis for the claim.

**Rationale:** The low entry cost ensures that access to justice is not gated by licensing fees. The royalty structure aligns the framework's sustainability with successful outcomes — the Burgess Principle is funded by the accountability it creates.

4. **Tier determination** — If there is ambiguity about which tier applies, the determination is made by the framework author based on the organisation's nature, scale, and intended use.
