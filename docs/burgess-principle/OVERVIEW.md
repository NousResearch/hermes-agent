## The Binary Test

The Burgess Principle poses a single, binary question at every point where a system acts on or about an individual:

> **"Was the individual considered as an individual human being, or were they processed as a unit within a system?"**

There are only two outcomes:

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **PASS** | The individual was considered as an individual human being. Their specific circumstances, context, and humanity were factored into the decision. |
| **FAIL** | The individual was processed as a unit within a system. They were treated as a case number, a data point, or an entry in a queue — without genuine individual consideration. |

There is no partial credit. The test is binary by design.

## UK Certification Mark UK00004343685

The Burgess Principle is registered as a UK Certification Mark (UK00004343685). This mark certifies that a process, product, or service has been evaluated against the binary test. Organisations that adopt the Burgess Principle and pass the certification criteria may display the mark to signal that their systems are designed to treat individuals as individuals.

## Why It Matters for AI Agents

AI agents like Hermes operate at scale. They make decisions, generate responses, and take actions that can affect real people. The risk of processing individuals as units — stripping away context, nuance, and humanity — scales with automation.

The Burgess Principle provides a concrete, auditable checkpoint:

1. **Before an automated decision is executed**, the agent asks whether the individual affected has been considered as an individual.
2. **If the answer is no**, the agent must flag the decision for human review or modify its approach to incorporate individual consideration.
3. **If the answer is yes**, the agent proceeds with a clear audit trail showing that individual consideration occurred.

### Application Areas

- **API Governance:** When an agent interacts with APIs that process personal data, the binary test ensures that API calls are not blindly batching individuals through systems without consideration.
- **Automated Processing:** Automated emails, notifications, account actions, content moderation decisions, and billing operations all carry the risk of treating people as units. The binary test catches this.
- **Decision-Making:** When an agent is asked to make recommendations, assessments, or classifications about individuals, the Burgess Principle requires that each person's individual circumstances are genuinely considered — not just pattern-matched against a ruleset.
- **Institutional Correspondence:** Any communication generated by an agent that addresses or concerns an individual must reflect individual consideration, not templated processing.

---

## Core Principles

1. **Binary, not graduated.** The test produces a PASS or FAIL. There is no spectrum. This prevents organisations from claiming partial compliance.
2. **Applied at the point of action.** The test is not a retrospective audit. It is a pre-action check that runs before the decision is executed.
3. **Universal scope.** The test applies to any system — public or private, digital or physical, automated or manual — that acts on or about an individual.
4. **Auditable.** Every application of the test produces a record: the decision description, the test result, and the reasoning.
