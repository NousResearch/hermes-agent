# Academic Placement Outreach

## Core principle

Treat academic placement search as a high-urgency placement pipeline, not a passive research task. Capture requirements, search approved partners first, research outside leads in parallel, rank by fit and approval speed, then send concise outreach with rapid follow-up.

## Workflow

### 1. Capture requirements and constraints

- Program, school, degree, location, deadline, start date, hour requirements.
- Target populations/practice areas.
- Approved portal or school partner list.
- Supervisor credential requirements.
- User background and credibility points.

### 2. Search approved partners first

Approved-school sites usually have the shortest paperwork path. Capture agency name, location, contact, opening status, requirements, supervision, and next action.

### 3. Research outside leads in parallel

Prioritize smaller/private/community agencies with direct contacts and visible practicum/internship language. Deprioritize bureaucratic public systems unless already approved.

### 4. Rank into tiers

#### Tier A — contact immediately

Strong fit, likely approved or fast-moving, direct contact, realistic supervision.

#### Tier B — contact after A

Good fit but contact/supervision/approval path unclear.

#### Tier C — backup

Slower, less aligned, or no direct contact path.

## Outreach

Use concise emails that include identity, school/program, practicum start timeframe, fit, relevant experience, and a direct ask for opportunities or the right coordinator.
