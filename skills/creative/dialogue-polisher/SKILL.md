# Screenplay Dialogue Polisher Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill when a user provides a flat, plain, or boring piece of script dialogue and wants to enhance it by giving the character a specific voice, attitude, or archetype.

## Procedure
1. Identify the raw dialogue and the desired character archetype requested by the user. (If no archetype is specified, default to a classic **"1940s Noir Detective"** vibe).
2. Rewrite the dialogue lines while preserving the original meaning, but completely change the vocabulary, slang, tone, and pacing to match the chosen archetype perfectly.
3. Present the result in a clean screenplay format block:
   - **CHARACTER NAME (ARCHETYPE)**
   - **Dialogue lines formatted cleanly.**
4. Provide a 1-sentence "Writer's Note" explaining why the specific word choices were made for that archetype.

## Pitfalls
- Do not change the core plot or the baseline message of what the character is trying to say. Only polish the style.
- Rely purely on your internal linguistic knowledge base. Do not use external network requests.

## Verification
The polished dialogue must sound distinctly different from the raw text and carry the unmistakable traits of the chosen archetype.
