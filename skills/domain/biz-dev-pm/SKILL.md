# Business Development PM (biz-dev-pm)

A comprehensive framework for designing **automated high-ticket sales systems** based on human psychology and behavioral economics. Optimized for solo knowledge businesses and platform businesses.

---

## 1. Core Principles

- **Automated Revenue > Labor Revenue** — The goal is "a system that runs without me"
- **Desire > Need** — Don't explain necessity; make them *want* it
- **Instinct First, Thinking Later** — No abstract explanations; intuitive hooking only
- **Group > Individual** — 1:N structures, not 1:1
- **10x Price > 10x Customers** — Raising price is 10x easier than growing customer count

---

## 2. Six Immutable Human Traits

The foundation of all theories. Permanent business principles unchanged for centuries.

| # | Trait | Core | Business Application |
|---|-------|------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Need Illusion** | Confuse wants with needs | Connect to money/health/relationships + create "disappointment crisis" |
| 2 | **Self-Infallibility** | Cannot admit being wrong | Leverage sunk cost; intentionally raise purchase barriers |
| 3 | **Herd Dependence** | Groups move uniformly | 1:N products, hierarchy design, pre-form groups |
| 4 | **Monopoly Virtue** | Monopolize but wrap in altruism | VIP exclusive benefits + secrecy |
| 5 | **Efficiency Priority** | Brain prioritizes energy saving | Intuitive hooking → limit choices → impulse purchase |
| 6 | **Free-Taking** | Chase freebies; favor ≠ favor | Free bait to attract + maintain high-value positioning |

---

## 3. The Six-Step Sales Method

A systematic framework for knowledge product sales.

```
① Show or tell how great the experience is
② Provide for free or at nominal cost
③ Build dependency until they voluntarily demand more
④ Customers beg you to sell to them
⑤ Sell at premium prices
⑥ Repeat ①–⑤ and collect revenue
```

**Key insight:** Create the *feeling of wanting* first, and price becomes irrelevant.

## 4. Content & Copywriting

### 4-1. Subconscious Copywriting — Six Boxes

Techniques for crafting copy that targets the instinctive system. Mix and match as needed.

| # | Box | Core | Modifiers / Techniques |
|---|-----|------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Desire** | Connect to money/health/relationships | Leverage the 3 survival essentials |
| 2 | **Effortless** | Feels like getting something for nothing | "easy and simple", "free", "immediately", "learn once, use forever" |
| 3 | **Shock** | Insert shocking expressions | Counterintuitive, innovative, provocative, dangerous |
| 4 | **Scarcity** | Package as rare/precious | Secret, hard-won, limited, novel |
| 5 | **Specificity** | Narrow the thinking scope | Numbers/criteria (concrete), "this one" (pointing), "formula/law" (structured) |
| 6 | **Mystery Name** | Use unfamiliar naming | Unique terms → curiosity + memorability. Never use alone |

**Example Transformations:**
- ❌ "How to do overseas dropshipping" → ✅ "How to make a fortune selling other people's products"
- ❌ "Knee rehabilitation methods" → ✅ "How to keep 20-year-old knees for life"

### 4-2. Neuroscience Content Formula — Six Stages

A structural template applicable to all content (videos, articles, courses, product pages).

| Stage | Name | Role | Key Point |
|-------|------|------|-----------|
| ① | **Main Hook** | "Wait, what's this?" | 5% of content but **most important**. Thumbnail/title/first line |
| ② | **Sub Hook** | "Why should I keep reading?" | Communicate benefits + anchoring (unique voice creates emotional memory) |
| ③ | **Proof of Effect** | "It actually works!" | No lengthy explanations → intuitive visuals. Leverage others' results if needed |
| ④ | **Expert Positioning** | "This person really knows their stuff!" | Main body = most volume but **lowest importance** |
| ⑤ | **Call to Action** | "I just need to do this!" | Easy, simple next mission. Never directly push subscribe/buy. Provider stance |
| ⑥ | **Sub Hook** | "I absolutely must do this!" | Final emotional impact. Last emotion stays longest (Peak-End Rule) |

## 5. Fifteen Automation Theories

### 5-1. One-Way Street Theory

`"Easy entry, difficult journey."`

- Customer moves forward on a designed one-way road with no retreat
- Longer road → more investment → higher price acceptance
- Place "breadcrumbs" (free value) along the way to prevent drop-off
- **Minimize** product variety (1 is ideal) — too many choices kill purchases
- No mid-priced products → premium-only at the final gate

### 5-2. N-Graph Theory (Storytelling)

`Expectation ↗ → Setback ↙ → Success ↗↗`

- **Expectation**: Barely taste success from a terrible starting point
- **Setback**: Major decline → maximizes emotional engagement
- **Success**: Achieve far greater success through your own method
- Message: "My success has causation, not luck" → builds trust

### 5-3. Declaration Theory (Red Ocean Leverage)

Leverage the leader's infrastructure without entering the red ocean directly.

**Step 1 — Maximize Demand Scale:**
- Emphasize **goals**, not methods ("dropshipping" → "become a $30K/month business owner")
- Avoid industry red ocean keywords
- Create proprietary terms (Mystery Name box) → monopoly

**Step 2 — Refine Targeting:**
- Let the **market define your target** (start after 1,000+ subscribers with data)
- Analyze what fans like across other channels/keywords → discover content and keywords

### 5-4. Free Pickaxe Theory

`"Make them experience the problem."`

| Role | Core |
|------|------|
| ① Entry Induction | Everything appears free. Remove all commercial signals |
| ② Investment Induction | Induce time/effort investment (abstract spending) → accumulate sunk cost |
| ③ Customer Testing | Observe customers experiencing problems for data collection. Surveys are meaningless |
| ④ Result Accumulation | Some customers succeed → excitement → new inflow + paid product foundation |

**Never:** mention paid products early, say "in the paid course...", use words "free/paid", commercial menu names

### 5-5. Aladdin Theory

Make the customer the **deserving underdog**. "You have the ability, just not the opportunity." → Leverages Self-Infallibility + Monopoly Virtue traits.

### 5-6. Puzzle Theory

Treat the customer as your **final puzzle piece**. "Your success is my success too" → converts doubt into conviction.

### 5-7. Olympus Theory

Form an elite **Olympus group of ≤10 members**.
- Select smart, high-execution members
- Openly favor and differentiate → creates thirst/inferiority/motivation in general customers
- Provide first paid product free to Olympus → their results prove product excellence

### 5-8. Bus Theory

A missed bus **truly** never comes back.
- No fake scarcity — real execution only
- Strategic sales pauses → reservation system → discount vouchers on reopen
- "Whoever sets the start and end holds the initiative" (Cutting Reset)

### 5-9. Echo Theory

Product praise echoes everywhere — free automatic advertising.
- No direct promotion
- **Echo Page**: Pinned comment → results + know-how column page (arranged in impressive order)

### 5-10. Doorbell Theory

Launch paid products only when customers are **clamoring for it**.
- Launch signals: community inquiries flooding, daily emails/DMs, applications pouring in
- **Never launch** before doorbells ring

### 5-11. White Tag Theory (Price Manipulation)

Three stages to make customers set premium prices themselves:

| Stage | Method |
|-------|--------|
| ① Raise Economic Threshold | Encourage high-amount mentions in community → desensitize to big numbers |
| ② Devalue Money | Youth/time/execution > money. "Buying is the easiest option" |
| ③ Create Dilemma | Ask customers to set prices + half price for chosen one → converges to premium |

### 5-12. Fixed Value Theory (Price Design)

Revenue per customer = fixed value; design everything else around it.
- 3-product price design: ① bundle discount ② limited-time discount ③ price increase notice
- Make the bundle appear cheapest
- Second digit: **7 or 8** recommended (9 is too common)

### 5-13. Panic Theory

Sudden disappointment crisis → panic → thinking system shutdown → impulse purchase.

| Device | Method |
|--------|--------|
| ① Premium Final Gate | Reveal price last. After application form, discover premium price |
| ② Consecutive Last Steps | Unexpected obstacles in sequence (late discount deadlines, confusing cutoffs) |
| ③ Forced Guilt | "What do you want from me?" first → "What have you done for me?" → guilt |

### 5-14. Absolute Being Theory

**Purchase a magnanimous image** from customers → absolute being reputation → sales explosion.
- Accept abstract resources (time/effort) as payment
- Donate all labor revenue (consulting/seminars) entirely
- 0% commission on mentee revenue → "spend $3K to buy $100K in reputation"

### 5-15. Chain Leverage Theory

One success triggers chain expansion.
- **Expansion Leverage**: Add new echoes to existing business (review contests, etc.)
- **Replication Leverage**: Previous business results become the new business item itself

## 6. Practical Tips

### 6-1. Variable Intelligence Theory (Brain Optimization)

| # | Method | Core |
|---|--------|------|
| 1 | No Burning Bridges | Can't do business under survival threat. Keep a job with 5+ hrs/day for self-development |
| 2 | Exercise & Work Same Space | 1 pull-up → 1 line written → activates thinking system |
| 3 | Music for Emotion Control | Low energy → EDM activation → lofi/jazz switch → focus in 30 min |
| 4 | Store Motivation Sources | Organize dream car/house links → browse regularly |
| 5 | Emotion Manual | Prepare coping strategies for periodic emotional swings in advance |

### 6-2. Knowledge Assembly Technique

```
Immediate idea capture → accumulate in document
→ (1) Idea cleanup (delete half, 1 week)
→ (2) Outline organization (first structure, 1 week)
→ (3) Flesh out (fill in outline order)
```

### 6-3. Prohibited Business Types

| Prohibited | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Businesses with many uncontrollable factors (e-commerce stores) | Manufacturing/distribution outside control → no time/labor freedom |
| View-dependent businesses (ad-revenue blogs/YouTube) | Algorithm outside control + forced daily production → must link to a business |

### 6-4. Five Practical Tips

1. **Book Selection**: Avoid future predictions, emotional self-help, co-authored. Prefer knowledge-transfer focused.
2. **Copy Training**: Read every ad + analyze and record why it hooked you
3. **Optimal Resignation**: Not "I can probably quit now" → "If I don't quit, my business suffers"
4. **E-books**: 3-5 lines per paragraph, middle-school vocabulary, free fonts, 3D mockups
5. **Sales Sites**: Sixshop, Imweb, Gumroad recommended. WordPress not recommended.

□ 11. Place panic devices — final gate + last steps + forced guilt
□ 12. N-Graph storytelling — expectation → setback → success
□ 13. Apply neuroscience formula — structure all content in 6 stages
□ 14. Build absolute being image — magnanimous reputation for sales amplification
□ 15. Chain leverage — expansion (review contests) + replication (new business)

### 📝 Content Writing Template (Neuroscience Formula)

```
[① Main Hook] — 5%
→ Intense first stimulus that only instinct responds to
→ Apply Subconscious Copywriting box combinations
→ (e.g., title/thumbnail/first line)

[② Sub Hook] — 5%
→ "If you watch this to the end, you'll gain ___"
→ Create emotional memory with unique voice/tone (anchoring)

[③ Proof of Effect] — 10%
→ Present results with visual materials (no lengthy explanations)
→ Leverage external results if you lack your own

[④ Expert Positioning (Main Body)] — 60%
→ Deliver substantive knowledge/know-how
→ Most volume but lowest importance
→ Purpose: Plant "this person is a real expert" perception

[⑤ Call to Action] — 10%
→ Present an easy, simple next mission
→ Never directly push subscribe/buy
→ Maintain provider (dominant) stance

[⑥ Sub Hook] — 10%
→ Final emotional impact (Kahneman: Peak-End Rule)
→ "If you don't do this, ___" or "Remember just this: ___"
```

### 💰 Price Design Process (White Tag + Fixed Value)

```
Step 1: Set Fixed Value
  → Total revenue per customer = fixed value
  → (e.g., fixed value = $3,000)

Step 2: Design 3 Products
  → Product A (standalone) = 60-70% of fixed value
  → Product B (standalone) = 50-60% of fixed value
  → Product C (standalone) = 40-50% of fixed value
  → Bundle (A+B+C) = fixed value (appears dramatically cheaper)

Step 3: Place Price Devices
  □ Bundle discount (significantly cheaper than individual sum)
  □ Limited-time discount (linked to Bus Theory)
  □ Price increase notice (create urgency)

Step 4: White Tag Groundwork
  □ Encourage high-amount mentions in community → raise economic threshold
  □ Plant "youth/time/execution > money" perception → devalue money
  □ Involve customers in pricing → converge on premium price

Step 5: Price Number Rules
  → Last digit 7 or 8 recommended (9 is too common)
  → Even if individual prices rise, keep bundle (fixed value) steady or slight increase only
```

### 🆓 Free vs. Paid Distinction Guide

| Free (Pickaxe) | Paid |
|----------------|------|
| Anyone can easily understand and use immediately | Difficult to understand or apply |
| Stimulating, motivational | Value maintained only if few know it |
| Quick visible results | Slow to show results |
| The tool (pickaxe) itself | Solutions (gloves, helmets, upgrades) |

**Absolute Rules:**
- Never use the words "free" or "paid" themselves
- Never expose paid product existence early on
- Never restrict content with "details in the paid version..."
- Design everything to *appear* entirely free
